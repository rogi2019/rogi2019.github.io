REACH OUT AND GIVE INTERNATIONAL, INC (ROG-I) was founded in 2008 by Ratna Karmakar, an educationist and a social worker, in New Jersey.  It is approved as a (501)(c)(3) tax-exempt organization in the US.  Its goals include fostering health and education amongst needy children, providing monetary and in-kind help, and constantly recruiting committed volunteers to teach kids English, math and computers.

MONITORING PROJECTS:   

 

We have volunteers in India who visit the places we sponsor and monitor the activities of the kids receiving funds to make sure that our efforts are put to legitimate usess.  We require that the individuals receiving money continue to do well in schools.  We try to talk to the teachers and mandate that the students furnish copies of their bi-annual report cards from their schools to us.  Champa Sarkar of Rotary club of Bidhannagar, Salt Lake has gracefully consented to monitor most of our activities along with her fellow Rotarian husband, Shyamal Sarkar.

 

We currently have taken on a few projects such as the following:

 

 

 

 

1.      TUTORING needy kids (Grades k - 6th) at New Brunswick Public Library, New Brunswick, NJ.  Our volunteers serve the kids at the library Saturday (12 - 5 PM) and Sunday (1 - 5 PM).  Need volunteers to run this project.  Please sign up if you can!

                   

2.      PRABARTAK is a West Bengal Government sponsored home for young adults.  It houses about 30 disabled male and female.  The live-in superintendent, Mr. Ganguly, seeks help with improvement/maintenance of the infra structure, I.e., painting walls, repairing beds etc.  We try to help them as our funds become available.

3.                  We are also seeking volunteers whod like to spend some time in Kolkata to work with these folks.  Please contact Reach Out and Give International if you have any interest.

3.  REHABILITATION CENTER FOR CHILDREN (RCFC)  RCFC is located in Behala, Kolkata.  The Rehabilitation Center for Children was established in 1973 by a benevolent Belgian couple. It treats disabled children free of cost.  Free surgery and post surgery care are provided by physicians and other professionals who offer pro bono services.  They are in constant need of money and medical equipments.  ROG-I pledges to help them as fund becomes available.

5.  RAMAKRISHNA MISSION, Asansol  It has opened a new building in a big plot of donated land in kanyapur, Asansol.  It has a lot of  ambitious projects.  ROG-I has so far donated clothes, books and toys for children whom the organization serves.

6.  Swamiji Sangha, Gobardanga, North 24 Parganas -  This is an institution which was started by a philanthropist universally known as Mastermasay.  This school is for boys only.  Most parents are day laborers or menial workers.  In spite of the poor economic background, the school has been relatively successful in educating the children.   Recently, the West Bengal government has sponsored it.  That means the teachers salaries are paid by the government.  But the school has a dorm which needs a lot of ongoing maintenance.  The dining hall and the kitchen need new roof, finished floors and potable water.  Money is badly needed to support the work.

7.  Food Distribution - Our volunteers work once a month (third Saturday of each month) with New Brunswick Islamic Center to distribute raw food to needy people in New Brunswick, NJ
